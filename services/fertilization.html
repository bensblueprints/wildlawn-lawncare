Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to fertilize a lawn in Georgia?

For warm-season grasses like Bermuda and Zoysia, the first application should happen in late February to early March (pre-emergent), with follow-up treatments every 6-8 weeks through October. Fescue lawns benefit from fall fertilization in September-November. Our program is timed to Georgia's specific climate cycles for maximum results.

What products do you use on my lawn?

We use professional-grade, slow-release fertilizers that feed your lawn steadily over weeks rather than all at once. Our weed control products are EPA-registered and applied by licensed technicians. We select products based on your soil test results and grass type to ensure the right nutrient balance.

Is your fertilization treatment safe for pets and children?

Yes. We use products that are safe for residential use when applied correctly. We recommend keeping pets and children off the lawn until the product dries (typically 1-2 hours). We always notify you before each application so you can plan accordingly.

How many applications are in your fertilization program?

Our standard program includes 5-7 applications per year, spaced throughout the growing season. This typically includes 1-2 pre-emergent treatments, 2-3 fertilizer + weed control applications, a summer treatment, and a fall winterizer. The exact number depends on your lawn's needs and soil test results.
