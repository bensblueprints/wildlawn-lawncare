Custom Fertilization Programs for Georgia Lawns

Science-backed nutrition tailored to your grass type and soil conditions.

Georgia's red clay soil is notoriously nutrient-poor and acidic. Without a proper fertilization program, even well-maintained lawns in Metro Atlanta will struggle with thin spots, weed invasion, and poor color. Wild Lawn's fertilization service addresses these challenges head-on with targeted nutrient applications designed for the specific conditions found throughout Cobb, Cherokee, and Fulton counties.

We tailor every program to your grass type. Bermuda and Zoysia lawns need heavy nitrogen feeding during the warm months, while Fescue requires careful fall fertilization to build root strength before winter. Many homeowners in Roswell and Kennesaw have mixed grass types that require a blended approach — and that's exactly the kind of local expertise Wild Lawn brings to every property.

Our multi-application programs include pre-emergent weed control in early spring, growth-boosting nitrogen in summer, and root-strengthening potassium in the fall. We also monitor soil pH and can recommend lime applications when acidity levels are holding your lawn back — a common issue for properties in Marietta and Smyrna built on heavy clay.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I start fertilizing my Atlanta lawn?

For warm-season grasses like Bermuda and Zoysia, the first application should go down in late March or early April once soil temps reach 65 degrees. Fescue lawns benefit from early fall fertilization in September. We'll time everything based on current conditions.

Is fertilization safe for kids and pets?

Yes. We use professional-grade products and follow all Georgia application guidelines. We recommend staying off the treated area until the product has been watered in and the lawn is dry, typically 24-48 hours.

How many applications per year does my lawn need?

Most Metro Atlanta lawns benefit from 4-6 applications spread across the growing season. This includes pre-emergent, growth-phase feeding, summer stress support, and fall root building. We'll customize the schedule for your specific grass type.

Will fertilizer help with weeds?

A well-fed, thick lawn is the best defense against weeds. Our programs include pre-emergent herbicide in spring to prevent crabgrass and other annual weeds. For existing weeds, we can add targeted spot treatments to your plan.
