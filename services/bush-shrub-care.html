Frequently Asked Questions

It depends on the species. Spring-blooming shrubs like azaleas should be pruned right after they finish flowering (usually late April to May). Evergreens like boxwood and holly can be trimmed in late spring through early fall. We schedule your pruning at the optimal time for each shrub type to protect blooms and promote healthy growth.

Most shrubs in Metro Atlanta benefit from 2-4 trimmings per year. Fast-growing varieties like ligustrum and privet may need quarterly attention, while slower growers like boxwood and holly can be maintained with 2-3 annual trimmings. We can set up a seasonal maintenance schedule tailored to your specific shrubs.

Yes. We specialize in rejuvenation pruning for overgrown shrubs. Depending on the species, we can do a hard cut-back (reducing the shrub by up to two-thirds) or a gradual reduction over multiple seasons. Most shrubs respond very well to rejuvenation and will fill back in with dense, healthy new growth within one to two growing seasons.

We will assess the condition of your shrubs during our visit. For common issues like leaf spot, scale insects, or winter damage, we can remove affected branches to prevent spread and improve plant health. For severe disease or pest problems, we will recommend a treatment plan or refer you to a certified arborist if specialized care is needed.
