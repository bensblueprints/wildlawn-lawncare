Expert Shrub Trimming & Hedge Maintenance

Precision pruning that keeps your landscape looking polished year-round.

Overgrown bushes and neglected shrubs can make even the most beautiful home look unkempt. Metro Atlanta's long growing season means that boxwoods, hollies, azaleas, and ornamental grasses can quickly get out of hand without regular attention. Wild Lawn's bush and shrub care service keeps every element of your landscape looking intentional and well-maintained.

Our trained crews understand the specific pruning needs of common Georgia shrubs. Azaleas, for example, should be pruned shortly after they bloom in spring, while Leyland Cypress hedges need careful shaping to maintain density. Homeowners in Marietta and Vinings rely on us to know the right technique and timing for every plant in their landscape.

Beyond aesthetics, proper pruning promotes plant health by improving air circulation and sunlight penetration. This is especially important in Atlanta's humid climate, where fungal diseases thrive in dense, overgrown shrubs. Regular trimming also prevents bushes from encroaching on walkways, windows, and foundations — a common issue for properties in Smyrna and Woodstock where landscapes mature quickly.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to trim shrubs in Atlanta?

It depends on the species. Spring-blooming shrubs like azaleas should be pruned right after flowering. Evergreen hedges like boxwood and holly can be trimmed in late spring and again in early fall. We'll advise on the ideal timing for your specific plants.

Can you handle severely overgrown bushes?

Absolutely. We specialize in renovation pruning for neglected shrubs. Some species can be cut back hard and will regrow beautifully, while others need a phased approach over a couple of seasons. We'll assess your plants and recommend the best strategy.

Do you remove the trimmings?

Yes, full debris cleanup and removal is included in every bush and shrub care service. We leave your property spotless — no piles of branches or clippings left behind.

How often should hedges be trimmed?

Most hedges in Metro Atlanta benefit from 2-3 trimmings per year. Fast growers like privet may need monthly attention during summer, while slower-growing boxwoods can be maintained with 2 trims per season.
