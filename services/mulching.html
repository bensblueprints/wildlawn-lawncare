Frequently Asked Questions

What type of mulch is best for Atlanta landscapes?

Hardwood mulch is the most popular choice for Metro Atlanta homes. It breaks down slowly, enriches the soil, and holds up well in our climate. Pine bark is great for acid-loving plants like azaleas and blueberries, while colored mulch adds a decorative touch that lasts longer visually.

How often should mulch be replaced?

We recommend a full mulch refresh once a year, typically in early spring. Some homeowners in Alpharetta and Smyrna opt for a light top-up in the fall as well to maintain a fresh appearance through the cooler months.

How deep should mulch be applied?

The ideal depth is 2-3 inches. Too thin and weeds break through easily; too thick and it can trap excess moisture against plant stems, leading to rot. We always apply mulch at the right depth and pull it back from tree trunks and plant bases.

Do you remove old mulch before applying new?

It depends on the condition. If existing mulch has decomposed to less than an inch, we can add fresh mulch on top. If there's excessive buildup, we'll remove the old material first to maintain proper depth and airflow.
