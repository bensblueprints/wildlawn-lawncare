Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between soft washing and pressure washing?

Soft washing uses low water pressure combined with specialized biodegradable cleaning solutions to safely clean delicate surfaces like vinyl siding, stucco, painted wood, and roofing. Pressure washing uses high-pressure water and is ideal for hard surfaces like concrete driveways, sidewalks, and stone patios. We use the right method for each surface to prevent damage.

Will pressure washing damage my surfaces?

Not when done by professionals. We carefully assess each surface and adjust pressure, nozzle type, and technique accordingly. Soft surfaces like wood siding get gentle soft washing, while concrete and stone get higher pressure. Our team is trained to avoid etching, stripping paint, or damaging any material on your property.

How often should I have my house and driveway pressure washed?

In Metro Atlanta, we recommend house washing at least once per year — ideally after spring pollen season (late April/May). Driveways and walkways benefit from annual cleaning as well. Properties surrounded by trees or in shaded areas may need more frequent service due to faster algae and mildew growth.

When is pollen season in Atlanta, and do you offer pollen removal?

Atlanta's pollen season typically runs from mid-March through early May, with pine pollen being the heaviest offender. The yellow coating covers everything — cars, homes, decks, driveways, and outdoor furniture. Yes, we offer targeted pollen removal services and many customers schedule their annual wash right after peak pollen season ends.
